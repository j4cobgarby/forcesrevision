Motion

Ways of showing motion
There are a lot of different ways of representing motion. One of the most common is using a unit of speed, for example m/s, or: the amount of metres this object will travel per second, if continuing at this exact speed. For example, if a car travels 500 metres in 27 seconds, then its speed is 500m รท 27s = 18.518m/s
Another way of showing motion is with a distance/time graph, which is covered soon.
Distance/Time graphs
A distance time graph plots the distance something is from an origin point, against the time.



So by looking at this graph, you can work out all of these
  • The total distance travelled
  • The speed at each point
